What is Verge?

Verge is an open source, community driven cryptocurrency that focuses on speed, technology, and most importantly, privacy. This is an unofficial website devoted to helping people understand what Verge is and why its so important in todays society.


Features

Verge has many built-in features with many more to come this year. Heres a quick run-down of the main features and what they do.


Tor and i2p

Tor (The Onion Router) is a service that allows you to hide your identity by moving your traffic between many Tor servers and encrypting your data. It cannot be traced back to you as they would see the data originating from one of the Tor servers.

SPV

SPV stands for "Simple Payment Verification" which is technology that makes transactions take just 5-10 seconds on average. It does this by instead of downloading a whole block, it verifies if a transaction is in a block and only has to download block headers which are much smaller than full blocks.

Wraith Protocol

Wraith Protocol allows for fast, secure transactions by using technology called stealth addresses. This allows desired transactions go to seperate stealth addresses which sends the transaction to the reciever. This transaction is hidden on the ledger and impossible to trace. With Wraith Protocol, this functionality can be turned on and off for either private or transparent transactions.

Atomic Swaps

Atomic Swaps allow Verge to be used in DEX's (Decentralised Exchanges) which let you to trade between Verge, and other coins that have atomic swaps enabled, on a blockchain. This means that a company like Bittrex or CoinBase dont own your coins but are instead stored in a decentralised exchange like crypto-bridge.

Mobile Security

Verge is the first and only cryptocurrency to have an Android wallet with Tor integrated. This allows for IP masking and anonymity on the move. An i2p Androd wallet is expected in Q1 2018.

RingCT

RingCT allows you to conceal how much you are sending over the blockchain. With ringCT enabled, when you send a transaction, more than one transaction is sent out of your address. These contain other funds from your address and act as dummy transactions to hide how much is actually being sent. The dummy transactions are then sent back to your address and the actual transaction is sent on to the reciever.


RSK Smart Contracts

RSK smart contracts allow Verge to utilise smart contracts on the blockchain. This will be enabled in Q2 this year and also allows Verge to handle 2000 transactions per second which is 20 times faster than what it is now. Below is an image which may help you understand what a Smart Contract is.

FAQ

Q: What are the best Verge exchanges?

A: Bittrex and Binance are the most popular and most reliable exchanges for Verge.

Q: How do I use Atomic Swaps?

A: There are many DEX's (Decentralised Exchanges) like crypto-bridge that allow you to use Atomic Swaps with Verge.

Q: Is Verge a Dogecoin fork?

A: No. While Verge was called Dogecoin Dark, it was actually forked from Bitcoin/Peercoin.
